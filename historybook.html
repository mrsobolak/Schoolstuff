About Me

I currently reside in Boston, Massachusetts and serve as a proud member of the Continental Congress (1775–1780), presiding as its President from May 1775 to October 1777. I had hoped to be named Commander in Chief of the Continental Army, but that honor went to my esteemed colleague George Washington - and I support his leadership fully.

I am one of the wealthiest men in all 13 Colonies, thanks to the thriving mercantile import/export business I inherited from my dear Uncle Thomas Hancock. It has been my privilege to use this fortune in service of the cause of American liberty.

My wife Dorothy and I are expecting our daughter soon - exciting times at the Hancock household!
